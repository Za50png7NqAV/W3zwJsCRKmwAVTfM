PAN Card – Complete Short Information
Point Short Information
What is PAN Card PAN is a permanent identification number used for income tax and financial transactions
Full Form of PAN Permanent Account Number
Issuing Authority Income Tax Department, Government of India
PAN Number Format 10-character alphanumeric code (Example: AAAAA1234A)
Fourth Character Meaning P-Individual, C-Company, F-Firm, H-HUF, T-Trust
PAN Card Validity PAN is valid for lifetime
Who Can Apply Individual, Company, Firm, Trust, HUF
Modes of Application Online and Offline
PAN Card Verification PAN number can be verified online
PAN Active / Inactive Status PAN becomes inactive if not linked with Aadhaar
PAN and Aadhaar Linking Linking PAN with Aadhaar is mandatory
e-PAN Digital PAN card available in PDF format
Time Required to Get PAN e-PAN: Few minutes | Physical PAN: 7–15 working days
Types of PAN Card Individual PAN, Company PAN, Firm PAN, Trust PAN, HUF PAN
PAN Card Uses ITR filing, banking, property, vehicle, investment, business
PAN Card and Job Required for salary account, TDS and Form 16
PAN Card and Business Mandatory for GST registration and tax filing
Minor PAN Card PAN can be issued to individuals below 18 years
Minor PAN Update Correction is required after attaining 18 years of age
PAN Card Correction Name, DOB, photo, signature and address can be corrected multiple times
PAN Number Change PAN number never changes
PAN Card Lost Reprint PAN can be applied with the same PAN number
Multiple PAN Cards Holding more than one PAN is illegal
PAN Card Fraud Prevention Write “For KYC Purpose Only” on PAN photocopies
Structure of PAN Card Number
Part Description
PAN Format AAAAA1234A
First Three Letters Alphabetic characters based on a predefined pattern
Fourth Letter Indicates PAN holder category (P-Individual, H-HUF, C-Company, F-Firm, A-AOP, T-Trust)
Fifth Letter First letter of the PAN holder’s surname
Next Four Digits Numeric characters from 0 to 9
Last Letter Check digit used for PAN validation
Uses of PAN Card
Purpose Use of PAN Card
Income Tax For filing Income Tax Return (ITR)
Banking For opening savings and current bank accounts
Cash Transaction For cash transactions above ₹10,000
Fixed Deposit For opening Fixed Deposit (FD)
Property For buying or selling immovable property
Vehicle Purchase For purchasing a car or any motor vehicle
Investment For opening Demat account and share market investment
Foreign Travel For obtaining foreign exchange for overseas travel
Employment For salary account, TDS deduction and Form 16
Loans & Credit For applying loans and credit cards
Business For GST registration and business tax filing
Insurance For high-value insurance policies
KYC For KYC verification in financial institutions
Government Work For government tenders and official contracts
